About Toni Morrison


Toni Morrison, born Choloe Ardelia Wofford, was born on Febuary 18th, 1931 and passed away August 5th, 2019. Throughout her life she worked as a novelist, essayist, editor, teacher, and professor emeritus at Princeton University. In 1965 Toni Morrison began working as an editor for Random House Publishing. Within two years she became a senior editor for fiction and was the first African American Woman to hold the position. During her time as an editor for Random House she helped bring other African American writers into more mainstream positions. The novels she wrote won many awards including the National Book Critics Circle Award, Song of Solomomon (1977), the Pulitzer Prize and the American Book Award, Beloved (1987). In 1993 she recieved the Nobel Peace Prize for literature.
